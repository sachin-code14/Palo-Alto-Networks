Employee Engagement & Satisfaction Analysis – Palo Alto Networks
1- Project Overview

Employee Engagement & Satisfaction Analysis – Palo Alto Networks is an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze employee engagement, satisfaction, work-life balance, burnout risk, and workplace stress. The dashboard transforms HR data into actionable insights that help organizations understand employee well-being, identify disengagement patterns, and support data-driven workforce decisions.

Through dynamic KPIs, interactive filters, and insightful visualizations, this dashboard enables HR professionals and business leaders to monitor employee engagement trends, evaluate burnout risks, and improve overall organizational performance.

2- Business Problem

Employee engagement and satisfaction have a direct impact on productivity, retention, and organizational success. HR teams often struggle to identify employees at risk of disengagement or burnout and understand the factors affecting workplace satisfaction.

This dashboard helps answer key business questions such as:

How engaged are employees across different job levels?
Which employee groups have the highest burnout risk?
How does work-life balance affect employee satisfaction?
Does overtime or business travel influence engagement?
How does engagement vary across tenure and current roles?
What indicators highlight potential employee disengagement?

5- Key Insights

Overall employee engagement remains healthy, with an Engagement Index of 4.09.
Burnout Risk Score is relatively low at 9%, indicating limited burnout across the workforce.
Employees with longer tenure show a gradual decline in engagement, particularly after 30+ years.
Engagement levels remain consistent across job levels, suggesting balanced employee experiences.
Employees who stayed with the company demonstrate slightly higher engagement than those who left.
Frequent business travel shows a small impact on engagement, while non-travel employees exhibit marginally lower engagement.
Approximately 12% of employees fall into the high stagnation risk category, highlighting opportunities for proactive HR interventions.
